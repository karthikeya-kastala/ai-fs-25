Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of all time. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, he captains the Indian national team in all formats of the game. Kohli is known for his aggressive batting style, strong leadership, and numerous records in both international and franchise cricket. Early Life and Career: Kohli was born in Delhi and showed an early interest in cricket. He began his career with the Delhi Under-15 team and later played for the Delhi Under-17 team. He led India to victory in the 2008 Under-19 Cricket World Cup, marking a turning point in his career. Kohli made his international debut for India in 2008. International Career: Kohli has established himself as a prolific run-scorer and record-breaker in all three formats of the game. He holds numerous batting records, including the most ODI centuries. Kohli led India to their first-ever Test series win in Australia. He is considered one of the fittest and most ambitious cricketers of his generation. Kohli captained India in Tests for a significant period, achieving notable success. He recently retired from T20I captaincy after leading India to victory in the 2024 T20 World Cup. Personal Life: Virat Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in 2017. They have two children. Kohli is known for his fitness, dedication to the sport, and strong work ethic. He has also been recognized for his philanthropic efforts. Awards and Recognition: Kohli has received numerous awards, including the Arjuna Award, Padma Shri, and Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award. He is also the first player to win all three ICC awards in a single year. Kohli is widely admired for his leadership, sportsmanship, and commitment to excellence.
